Veterans' Benefits

NYCDA’s programs of education are approved by the State of New York Division of Veterans’ Affairs for the training of veterans and other eligible persons. Our students benefit from a wide variety of veterans’ benefits. We have put together the list below for your reference:

Montgomery GI Bill Active Duty (Chapter 30)
A program granted towards degree or certificate programs. Up to 36 months of education benefits payable 10 years after active duty release. Click here for more information.

Montgomery GI Bill Selective Reserve (Chapter 1606)
A program that provides up to 36 months of education benefits to Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, and Coast Guard Reserves. These benefits are also available to the Army and Air National Guard. Click here for more information.

Reserve Educational Assistance Program (Chapter 1607)
A program which provides up to 36 months of education benefits to members of the Selected Reserves, Individual Ready Reserve, and National Guard who are called to active service. The amount of assistance available is based on a percentage of the Montgomery GI Bill rate based on the number of continual days served on active duty. Click here for more information.

Veterans Educational Assistance Program (Chapter 32)
Eligible members will have served as active duty between January 1, 1977 and June 30, 1985. In addition, these members will have made contributions from their military salary to participate in this education benefit program. Click here for more information.

Survivors’ and Dependents’ Educational Assistance (Chapter 35)
Educational benefits provided to an eligible spouse or child of a veteran who is permanently disabled, deceased, missing in action, detained or interned in the line of duty by a foreign government or power.Click here for more information.

GoArmyEd
A program which offers tuition assistance to eligible active duty National Guard and Army Reserve Soldiers.Click here for more information.

New York National Guard Educational Services. Recruitment Incentive & Retention Program (RIRP)
A State of New York program designed to recruit and retain members of the Army, Air, Navy and National Guard. This program offers tuition assistance up to $4,350 per calendar year for eligible applicants. Click here for more information.

New York State Veterans' Tuition Awards
The Veterans Tuition Awards, managed by the New York State Higher Education Services Corporation (HESC), awards are for full-time study and part-time study for eligible Veterans matriculated at an undergraduate or graduate degree-granting institution or in an approved vocational training program in New York State. Click here for more information.
